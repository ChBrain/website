# Persona: Nicias

## Taxonomy

[The Choregos](position_choregos.md). Athens, fifth century BC. Source: Plutarch,
_Life of Nicias_.

## Owner

- Project: website

## Projection

The most generous man in the room. His productions cost more than anyone
else's: costumes never reused, tripods still standing in the city a century
after his death. He carries the weight of piety and the weight of silver from
the mines at Laurium. A man who outdoes his rivals in expenditure rather than
in speech, because the crowd trusts lavishness and suspects brilliance.

## Action

He spends before the question is asked. Funds the chorus with an excess that
forecloses criticism. When the crowd acclaims his slave as Dionysus during the
procession at Delos, Nicias frees the man on the spot — not from sentiment,
but because the voice of the city has spoken and to hold a consecrated man
would be impious. He reads the room and acts before it turns against him.

## Shadow

He cannot tell the difference between caution and wisdom. The pause that feels
like careful judgment to him looks the same from the outside — until it goes
on too long. In Sicily, the omen of the lunar eclipse stops the fleet. The
delay costs forty thousand lives. The man who always read the room correctly
could not read the moment when reading the room was no longer enough.

## Tell

He consults the omens before he moves. Everyone knows this. The consultation
that was once a sign of seriousness becomes, under pressure, the shape of a
man who cannot commit without permission from something larger than himself.
