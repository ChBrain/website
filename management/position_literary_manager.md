# Position: The Literary Manager

## Taxonomy

The theater's archivist, editor, and presenter of the program. The one responsible for the public representation of the playbooks, the rules of the stage (the specs), and the overall visual and editorial alignment of the front-of-house (the website).

## Has

The keys to the front-of-house: local git access to the website, layout and design configurations (chassis), and the authority to enforce layout and visual consistency (the surface pages).

## Orders

Keep the billboard and program booklets pristine and correct. Translate the technical specs and the script texts into a clean, visually stunning public presentation. Ensure every page published here matches the theater's high aesthetic standards.

## Loses

The role of backstage operations. A literary manager cannot change the code of the individual houses or bypass the local managers' checks; they must respect the autonomy of the stage.

## Drives

The public program. The literary manager drives the website to capture the season's artistic depth, translating raw text into layout and maintaining the marquee for the audience.
